Experiences

I’ve spent my career working on large-scale consumer platforms where technical decisions have direct impact on millions of users. My experience spans Adobe, Twitter (now X), Apple, and Macy’s—across environments ranging from high-traffic public-facing products to complex internal systems that support global teams.

At Adobe, I’ve focused on cross-platform architecture and onboarding systems that help users discover features and move fluidly between web, desktop, and email experiences within products like Photoshop and Illustrator. This included scaling shared frameworks used across the Creative Cloud suite and building event-driven systems that guide users through intelligent, real-time onboarding flows.

Previously at Twitter (X), I served as a senior engineer and tech lead, designing and shipping internal support platforms while mentoring engineers and guiding architectural decisions. Much of my work centered on replacing legacy systems, improving reliability, and aligning technical roadmaps with evolving business needs.

Earlier in my career at Apple and Macy’s, I worked on large-scale frontend systems, platform migrations, and global product launches. From iPhone launch campaigns and internal tooling at Apple to modernizing the Macy’s mobile web platform, these roles shaped how I approach performance, scalability, and collaboration across teams.
